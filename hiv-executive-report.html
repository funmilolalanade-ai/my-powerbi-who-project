# Global HIV Trends & Treatment Effectiveness Analysis ## Executive Summary This healthcare analytics project examines 25 years of WHO HIV data (2000–2024) to evaluate global trends in HIV infections, mortality, treatment access, and survival outcomes. Using Power BI, Power Query, DAX, and a Star Schema data model, the project transformed raw public health data into an interactive decision-support dashboard designed for healthcare stakeholders, policymakers, and public health organisations. --- ## Dashboard Overview [Insert Dashboard Screenshot] The dashboard consists of four analytical views: * Overview & KPIs * Geographic Analysis * Deep Dive Analysis * Deep Dive & Key Findings Interactive slicers allow users to analyse trends by year, country, and continent. --- ## Business Problem Despite significant investments in HIV prevention and treatment programmes globally, policymakers require clear evidence regarding: * Long-term HIV infection trends * Treatment effectiveness * Mortality reduction * Geographic disease burden * Resource allocation priorities This project was developed to provide evidence-based answers to these questions. --- ## Key Findings ### 1. HIV Infections Continue to Decline Global new HIV infections have declined significantly since 2000, demonstrating the impact of prevention programmes and expanded treatment access. ### 2. HIV Mortality Has Reduced Substantially HIV deaths have fallen dramatically from their historical peak, reflecting improvements in diagnosis, treatment availability, and patient management. ### 3. ART Coverage Continues to Increase Global ART coverage has reached over 77%, representing major progress in treatment accessibility. ### 4. Treatment Access Improves Survival Countries with higher ART coverage consistently demonstrate lower mortality rates and better survival outcomes. ### 5. Geographic Burden Remains Uneven Sub-Saharan Africa continues to account for the majority of global HIV infections, highlighting the need for sustained intervention and investment. --- ## Technical Solution ### Data Model Star Schema Architecture: * Fact_HIV * Dim_Indicator * Dim_Location * Dim_Year ### Technologies Used * Power BI * Power Query * DAX * Azure Maps * Data Modelling * Healthcare Analytics * Data Storytelling ### Analytical Features * Dynamic KPI Cards * Year-over-Year Analysis * Mortality Rate Calculations * Survival Rate Metrics * Geographic Heat Mapping * Risk Country Identification --- ## Strategic Recommendations 1. Increase ART coverage in countries below 80%. 2. Prioritise high-burden countries with elevated mortality rates. 3. Continue investment in prevention programmes. 4. Expand data-driven monitoring frameworks. 5. Use healthcare analytics dashboards to support funding and policy decisions. --- ## About the Analyst Olufunmilola Lanade Healthcare Data Analyst Enthusiast This project was completed as part of the 10Alytics Healthcare Data Analytics Specialisation programme and demonstrates practical application of healthcare business intelligence, epidemiological analysis, and data storytelling.
