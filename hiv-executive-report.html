Project Overview

This Power BI healthcare analytics project evaluates global HIV trends, treatment access, mortality rate, survival rate and public health outcomes using WHO data from 2000–2024. The project was designed to support evidence-based public health monitoring and strategic healthcare decision-making.

Dashboard Overview

Key KPIs

  • New Infections: 1.0M
  • HIV Deaths: 523K
  • People Living with HIV: 35M
  • ART Coverage: 77.2%
  • Mortality Rate: 1.49%
  • Survival Rate: 98.51%

Key Insights

  • Global HIV infections continue to decline, showing progress in prevention and treatment interventions.
  • HIV-related deaths have reduced significantly, reflecting improved treatment access and ART expansion.
  • ART coverage is a critical public health indicator because countries with stronger treatment access generally show lower mortality.
  • Africa remains the highest-burden region and requires continued prevention, treatment and funding support.
  • High-risk countries should be prioritised for intervention where infection burden remains high and treatment access is weaker.

Business Impact

The dashboard provides public health stakeholders with a clear monitoring framework for tracking HIV burden, identifying high-risk countries, assessing treatment effectiveness and supporting evidence-based resource allocation.

Recommendations

  • Prioritise countries with high infection burden and low ART coverage.
  • Use ART coverage as a core treatment effectiveness KPI.
  • Monitor mortality and survival rates annually to assess healthcare intervention impact.
  • Increase investment in prevention programmes in high-burden regions.
  • Use interactive dashboards to support public health reporting and policy decisions.
